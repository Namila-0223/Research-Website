Literature Survey

Sri lanka is the 4th largest coconut exporter in the world [1]

Figure 1. Annual coconut production 2011 - 2021 [1]

Research on nature-based and aesthetic interventions for stress reduction uses facial data to objectively measure stress levels, addressing the pervasive issue of stress-related health problems in modern fast-paced lifestyles. By integrating technology, psychology, and environmental factors, this study aims to develop innovative strategies for enhancing wellness and understanding how nature and aesthetics positively affect stress responses. One study utilized voice analysis to identify interview stress, employing machine learning with features like mean energy, mean intensity, and Mel-Frequency Cepstral Coefficients (MFCCs). The method analyzed speech spectrograms, deriving secondary features with sigma-pi neuron cells across three frequency band types. Using the SUSAS database, the k-nearest neighbor (KNN) method and Gaussian mixture model (GMM) classifiers achieved a best classification accuracy of 67.84% on the ERB scale.

Another review examined stress detection using smartphones, smartwatches, and wearables in daily life, categorizing studies by physiological methods and various settings. While most research occurs in controlled environments, this survey highlighted the potential and challenges of real-life stress detection technologies. Additionally, a system for detecting stress in IT professionals was proposed, utilizing image processing and machine learning. Faces were detected with MTCNN, features extracted with FaceNet, and stress classified using SoftMax, achieving an 85% accuracy. This system shows potential for improving IT professionals' well-being by helping them identify and manage stress.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Identification & classification

There are no records of a smart solution for coconut pest and disease identification and classification in Sri Lanka. Symptoms of coconut disorders show similar characteristic appearance and therefore it is a challenge to provide a solution.

Severity assessment

Smart solution for identification of some coconut pest and diseases is reported in India but assessment on severity of disease conditon and progression level of pest damage is not attempted.

Information sharing

Real time communication system to speed up information sharing between coconut growers, extension personals and researchers has been identified as priority need for effective pest and disease control.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

How can real-time face and speech data analysis be used to identify and reduce stress levels in the modern world? How successful are nature-based and artistic interventions in reducing stress as these technical evaluations suggest?

The research problem is how to effectively identify and treat stress using machine learning techniques combined with nature-based and artistic interventions. Specifically, it aims to use voice and facial expression analysis to detect stress, then offer personalized treatments such as art therapy and outdoor activities. The goal is to develop a system that continuously monitors stress levels and improves its recommendations based on long-term data and individual preferences.


Proposed Solution

Stress is a pervasive issue affecting various contexts, from daily life to high-pressure professional environments. Traditional stress management often relies on self-reported data, which can be inaccurate. Advances in technology offer the potential for real-time stress detection using facial and voice recognition, providing personalized interventions.
Facial expressions, such as lip and eyebrow movements, can reveal stress levels. By analyzing these minute variations, sophisticated models can detect stress. Implementing non-intrusive data collection methods, like using cameras during breaks, can enhance this process. This continuous learning approach aims to create reliable real-time stress detection tools to support high-stress professionals and individuals in managing their stress.
Voice data also plays a crucial role in detecting stress through changes in pitch, intensity, and rhythm. Overcoming challenges like background noise requires noise reduction techniques and stress-reflective acoustic features. Efficient real-time processing algorithms are essential for providing quick feedback, which is crucial for stress management apps. Nature-based and aesthetic interventions have shown promise in reducing stress. This study evaluates their effectiveness, especially when personalized based on real-time detection. Incorporating multimedia elements, such as calming images and ambient sounds, can enhance the stress-relief experience. Personalized content tailored to individual preferences and stressors can make interventions more immersive and effective.
Combining facial and voice data with time series forecasting models, such as ARIMA, allows for predicting stress levels over time. This involves continuously monitoring multimodal data to identify stress patterns, enabling proactive, personalized interventions. The goal is to develop a comprehensive stress management system that offers timely interventions and improves overall well-being.
The project aims to integrate these elements into a unified system capable of real-time stress detection, immediate assessment of aesthetic and nature-based treatments, and future stress level prediction. This approach leverages modern technologies to provide efficient, personalized stress management, advancing mental health and adaptability across various contexts.

Methodology

Figure 2. High Level Architecture of the system.

The proposed pest and disease Surveillance system consists of 4 main components. They are;

  1. WCLWD and its symptom severity identification
  2. CCI Identification and progression level determination
  3. Deficiency Identification (Mg deficiency and Leaf Scorch Decline)
  4. Water resource identification
  5. Crowdsourcing for information sharing.

Fig 4 illustrates the overall system diagram of the proposed solution which was intended to provide a smart approach for stakeholders, researchers, and Coconut Development Officers (CDOs) to detect the coconut diseases and pest infestations that may affect the coconut industry. As shown in the diagram, the registered users of the system can capture or upload the images which are suspicious. The images are sent to the Amazon Web Services (AWS) backend server where the flask server is deployed. These images are processed in the flask server by the designed DCNN models for disease identification. If WCLWD is found, the symptom severity will be determined using CNN models. Simultaneously, if CCI is identified, the images will go through the Mask-R-CNN model to determine the progression level while the number of caterpillars are extracted using the YOLOv5 object detection algorithm. Images will be classified using the CNN models of Mg deficiency and LSD at the same time.

Once the system identifies that the leaves are infected, then the response will be captured by the crowdsourcing platform. The Google Map will be updated with the real time locations (latitude and longitude) of the infected palms. In Addition, the system will automatically send notifications to the farmers and other stakeholders who are at the risk of infection.
